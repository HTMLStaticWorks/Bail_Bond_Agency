Collateral Policy & Release Guidelines

We prioritize transparency. Below is an authoritative explanation of what constitutes collateral, how we secure it, and how to request its return.

Understanding Collateral

Collateral is physical or financial equity provided by the cosigner to secure the bond. It acts as a safety net ensuring that if the defendant skips court, the financial value of the bond can be covered.

Crucial Fact: Not all bonds require collateral. For qualified residents with solid credit history, stable employment, and smaller bond amounts (under $10,000), we frequently issue bonds with "no collateral required," backed solely by your signature.

Secure Audited Custody

All collateral items—deeds, cash logs, bank locks—are held in secure accounts or physical vaults. Our agency is fully bonded, guaranteeing that your assets remain safe and unchanged.

Acceptable Forms of Security

If collateral is required under underwriting guidelines, our agency accepts the following formats:

Real Estate

Homes, land, or commercial properties with verified equity. A temporary lien is recorded with the county clerk and removed after case resolution.

Vehicles

Cars, trucks, or trailers with clean titles, no active liens, and equity exceeding the bond value. Titles are held in our secure vault.

Liquid Funds

Cashier's checks, certificate of deposit (CD) holds, or funds deposited directly into our secure escrow holding account.

Valuables

Certified gold, silver bullion, or fine jewelry. Evaluated by professional appraisers and stored in secure safety deposit boxes.

The Return Process (Step-by-Step)

Once the case is completed, your collateral must be legally returned. Follow these steps to initiate release.

1

Secure Court Disposition

Request a physical copy of the "Certificate of Disposition" or "Case Dismissal Order" stamped by the Court Clerk. This serves as the legal proof that the defendant's bail bond liability is discharged.

2

Submit Release Request

Deliver the court document to our headquarters or upload it securely through the client portal. Our compliance department will review court files to verify that all conditions have been met.

3

Outstanding Balance Check

Our billing group reviews the account log to ensure the 10% premium has been paid in full. If an active payment plan is still running, it must be settled before physical release of collateral occurs.

4

Physical Asset Return

Lien release filings are sent to the county recorder, vehicle titles are mailed, and jewelry/cash holds are returned. This release is finalized within 15 to 21 business days of verification.
