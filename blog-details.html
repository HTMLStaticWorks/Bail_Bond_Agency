Immediate Steps After a Loved One is Detained

By Arthur Vance, L.B. June 18, 2026 5 Min Read

Learning that a family member or friend has been arrested is a highly stressful event. It is natural to feel panic, but acting on impulse can lead to procedural errors or unnecessary costs. Below is a structured, calm checklist from our senior bondsmen on how to proceed.

1. Identify the Correct Detention Facility

The first step is determining exactly where the individual is held. In most municipal regions, there are separate city lockups (managed by municipal police departments) and county jail facilities (managed by the County Sheriff). Booking listings may take several hours to update online. If you are unsure, do not call multiple jails repeatedly; call our agency office, and we will check our local law databases immediately.

2. Retrieve Key Booking Information

Before any bondsman or attorney can file paperwork, they will require basic facts about the detainee. Write down the following details as soon as they become available:

  • The defendant's full legal name (including correct spelling) and date of birth.
  • The jail facility location where they are housed.
  • The booking identification number or inmate case number.
  • The exact charges listed and the bail amounts set for each charge.
Critical Caution: Recorded Jail Calls

All phone calls placed from jail cells or booking areas are recorded and monitored by law enforcement. Do NOT discuss case details, charges, or factual events during these calls. Anything said can be requested by county prosecutors and used in court trials.

3. Coordinate With a Licensed Agency

Avoid paying the full bail amount in cash directly to the court unless you have ample liquid assets that you can afford to lock up for months. Posting cash directly means the court retains those funds until the trial concludes, which can take over a year. Engaging a licensed bail bondsman means you only pay the 10% statutory premium, freeing up your capital during the trial.

If you have any questions, keep a copy of this guide on hand and call our 24/7 support line for immediate assistance.
