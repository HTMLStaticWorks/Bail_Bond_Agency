Understanding the 10% Statutory Premium

In almost all states, bail bond rates are strictly regulated by the State Department of Insurance. The legal premium rate is typically fixed at **10% of the total face value of the bail bond** (some jurisdictions permit 8% or 15% under specific scenarios).

This premium is the cost of buying a surety bond. It acts as the insurance fee and is non-refundable once the bond is filed and the defendant is released. This fee is earned by the agency to cover the administrative underwriting risks of the bond.
