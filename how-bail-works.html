Understanding the Bail System

Education is our first priority. We break down the legal steps between an arrest and final release so you can make informed decisions.

The Arrest-to-Release Timeline

While the court system involves many administrative layers, the core journey follows five distinct checkpoints.

01

Arrest & Booking

Following an arrest, the individual is transported to a municipal or county jail facility. Booking officers record fingerprints, photograph details, and enter charges into the county database. This process takes 2 to 6 hours.

02

Bail Setting

Bail is determined either by a pre-set bail schedule (based on charges) or during a bail hearing before a magistrate within 24 hours. The judge weighs the severity of the charges, past record, and community ties to determine the financial amount.

03

Contacting Apex Bail Bonds

Once bail is set, the family or cosigner contacts our agency. We retrieve the booking records directly, analyze options, and coordinate signatures and payments (either online or in office).

04

Posting the Bail Bond

Our licensed bondsman visits the detention center clerk to present the physical bond. The clerk verifies the credentials, files the receipt, and processes the jail release forms.

05

Release and Court Compliance

The defendant is released. They must attend all scheduled hearings, check-ins, and legal steps. Once the judge issues the final trial verdict or case dismissal, the bond is discharged by the court.

Cosigner & Defendant Agreements

Every bail bond contract establishes separate legal responsibilities. Understanding these rules protects both parties from financial risk.

Defendant Obligations

The person who was in custody has legal mandates their freedom:

  • Must attend all courtroom proceedings without delay.
  • Must check in weekly with the assigned Apex agent.
  • Must seek court authorization before changing residence.
  • Must comply with any court conditions (e.g. curfew).

Cosigner (Indemnitor) Obligations

The guarantor holds the financial and moral responsibility of the bond:

  • Guarantees the defendant's compliance with all court dates.
  • Remains responsible for paying any unpaid premium balances.
  • Assumes full liability for the entire face value of the bail.
  • Must provide correct contact details and address updates.

What Happens if a Court Date is Missed?

Missing court ("failure to appear" or FTA) triggers immediate legal steps. However, not all missed dates are intentional (e.g. medical emergencies, transport failures). If you miss a date:

Action Step: Call Us Immediately

Contact Apex right away. We can often file a "consent of surety" or coordinate with your attorney to reschedule the warrant before law enforcement gets involved. Acting quickly prevents arrest.

Consequences of Fleeing

  • Court issues bench warrant.
  • Bail bond is declared in forfeiture.
  • Cosigner assets/collateral used to pay court.
  • Additional criminal charges filed.
